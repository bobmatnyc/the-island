## Research Methodology

### Source Reliability Tiers

All sources are classified into 5 tiers based on reliability:

1. **Tier 1 - Court Documents** (highest reliability)
   - Depositions, trial testimony, court exhibits, judicial orders
   - Examples: Federal criminal convictions, civil lawsuit settlements

2. **Tier 2 - Investigative Journalism** (high reliability)
   - Miami Herald, New York Times, Wall Street Journal, ProPublica, NPR, BBC
   - Verified reporting from established news organizations

3. **Tier 3 - Public Records** (verified)
   - Flight manifests, property records, SEC filings, corporate registrations
   - Government databases and official records

4. **Tier 4 - Biographical Databases** (general)
   - Wikipedia, Britannica, Forbes, IMDb
   - Publicly available reference materials

5. **Tier 5 - Secondary Sources** (requires verification)
   - News aggregators, blogs, social media
   - Not used without corroboration from higher tiers

### Verification Standards

- **Biographical facts**: Minimum 2 independent sources
- **Epstein connections**: Prefer Tier 1 court documents or Tier 3 flight logs
- **All sources**: Must be dated and accessible
- **Unverified information**: Clearly flagged for human review

### Ethical Guidelines

**Include Only**:
- Publicly reported information
- Court-documented facts
- Verified journalism from reputable sources
- Public statements by entities themselves
- Public records accessible to anyone

**Explicitly Exclude**:
- Unverified allegations
- Social media rumors
- Tabloid speculation without verification
- Information about minors (unless adults who publicly identified)
- Private information not in public record
